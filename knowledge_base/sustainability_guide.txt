SUSTAINABLE LIVING GUIDE

CARBON FOOTPRINT BASICS
The average person in developed countries produces 10-20 tons of CO2 per year.
Transportation accounts for 29% of emissions, with single-occupancy vehicles being the largest contributor.
Home energy use contributes 20% of personal carbon footprint.
Food production, especially meat and dairy, accounts for 15% of personal emissions.

SUSTAINABLE TRANSPORTATION
- Walking or cycling: 0 kg CO2 per km
- Public transit: 0.05 kg CO2 per km
- Electric vehicle: 0.05-0.1 kg CO2 per km
- Hybrid vehicle: 0.12 kg CO2 per km
- Gasoline car: 0.19 kg CO2 per km

SUSTAINABLE DIET
- Plant-based diet: 1.5 tons CO2/year
- Vegetarian diet: 1.7 tons CO2/year
- Pescatarian diet: 2.0 tons CO2/year
- Meat-eating diet: 2.5 tons CO2/year
- Buy local and seasonal produce to reduce transportation emissions

ENERGY EFFICIENCY
- LED bulbs use 75% less energy than incandescent
- Smart thermostats can reduce heating/cooling costs by 10-23%
- Energy Star appliances use 10-50% less energy
- Solar panels can offset 80-100% of home electricity use

WASTE REDUCTION
- Composting reduces landfill waste by 30%
- Recycling aluminum saves 95% of energy needed for new production
- Reusable water bottles prevent 156 plastic bottles per person per year
- Cloth shopping bags prevent 500 plastic bags per person per year

SUSTAINABLE HABITS
- Reduce shower time by 2 minutes: saves 10 gallons of water daily
- Unplug devices when not in use: saves 5-10% on electricity
- Buy second-hand clothing: reduces fashion industry emissions by 50%
- Use cold water for laundry: saves 90% of washing machine energy
